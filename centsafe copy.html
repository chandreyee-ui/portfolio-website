Centsafe- UX/UI Case Study | Fintech

Overview:

Centsafe is a mobile application designed to help users save money efficiently for significant purchases, such as weddings, vacations, or new cars. The app offers tools to track income and expenses, set and monitor savings goals.

Designed with simplicity and trustworthiness in mind, Centsafe aims to empower users who may not be financially savvy to take control of their savings journey.

Purpose and Context:

Centsafe was developed during my CareerFoundry course to address the challenge of quickly saving money for a specific goal. Targeted at users familiar with technology but uncomfortable managing finances, the goal was to simplify saving while boosting user confidence and control.

Objective:

In today’s fast-paced world, saving money for a big purchase can feel like trying to fill a leaky bucket. Enter Centsafe, a responsive web and mobile app designed to make saving as simple and stress-free as possible. Whether you're planning a dream vacation, buying a new car, or preparing for a wedding, Centsafe helps you take control of your finances, providing the tools and guidance needed to reach your savings goals with confidence. With a focus on clarity and trust, Centsafe transforms the daunting task of managing finances into an empowering experience.

Role:

As the sole UX/UI Designer, I was responsible for conducting comprehensive user research, developing wireframes, and iterating on designs based on usability testing.

Tools

  • Figma
  • Adobe Photoshop

Duration:

July 18- August 10, 2024

Design Process:

The design process for Centsafe followed a user-centered approach, focusing on understanding user needs and creating a solution that addresses their challenges effectively.

Research:

My first step was diving into competitive analysis, exploring existing financial apps like Mint, YNAB, and Qapital. I wanted to understand what these apps were doing right, and more importantly, where they were falling short in helping users save for specific goals.

I conducted user interviews to uncover the pain points that people experience when trying to save money. The insights were eye-opening—many users felt overwhelmed by financial jargon and frustrated by the lack of personalized guidance.

User Stories and Flow Development:

From these insights, I developed user stories that captured the essential features Centsafe neededto have. For example, one user story focused on the ability to set up an autosave feature that would automatically transfer a set amount of money into savings on a regular basis. This feature was crucial for users who wanted to save consistently without the hassle of manual transfers.

I then created a user flow diagram, mapping out the key interactions within the app. This step wasvital in ensuring that users could navigate Centsafe effortlessly, from setting up their profile to tracking their savings progress.

  1. Through competitive analysis, I identified key features like automated savings, personalized financial advice, and user-friendly dashboards as essential in similar apps
  2. This insight led to the development of Centsafe as a secure, user-friendly app that prioritizes clear, personalized guidance
  3. By focusing on intuitive design and trustworthiness, Centsafe directly addresses the needs of users who seek a reliable financial partner without the complexity
  • “As a money-saver, I need to input information on my income and expenses so that I can see an overview of my finances.”
  • “As a user, I want to set up an autosave feature that automatically saves a custom amount at my chosen frequency, so I can effortlessly stay on track with my savings goal.”
  • “As a user, I want to see a clear, visual dashboard of my finances so that I can quickly understand how much I’m spending and saving.”
  • “As a user, I need to track my progress toward my savings goal, with regular updates and milestones, so I stay motivated and on track.”

Userflow:

Wireframing and Prototyping:

With the foundation laid, I moved on to wireframing. The low-fidelity wireframes helped me sketch out the basic structure and functionality of the app, focusing on user experience and ease of use. As I progressed to mid- and high-fidelity wireframes, I began refining the visual design. My goal was to create an interface that was not only intuitive but also visually appealing, with a calming color palette and clear, readable typography that made financial data easy to digest.

Final Designs:

Homepage:

The homepage serves as a central hub with sections for Total Balance, Unallocated Savings,Income, Expenses, and Transaction History. Key features are easily accessible through intuitive icons and a concise navigation bar, making browsing straightforward.

Goals dashboard:

The goals dashboard manages all savings objectives. It displays the available funds that can be allocated towards goals, providing a clear overview for users. The page also offers easy navigation to view ongoing or completed goals, with a prominent option to create new goals, making it simple to track progress and initiate new savings plans.

Goals page:

The goals page allows users to add more savings, edit their goal, and track progress with real-time updates on elapsed time, accumulated savings, and the remaining amount. It also includes the Auto-Save feature, and suggested autosave options.

Autosave page:

The Auto-Save feature page introduces users to the convenience of automated savings, allowing them to set up recurring contributions toward their goals. On the next page, users can easily customize the amount and frequency of these automatic transfers, ensuring their savings plan fits their financial needs and timeline.

Reflection:

Testing and iterating:

User testing was a critical phase. I conducted sessions with a diverse group of users to gather feedback on both the design and functionality. One key finding was the importance of making the autosave setup process as simple and transparent as possible.

Based on the feedback, I iterated on the design, enhancing the clarity of the autosave feature, adding a “How it works” section and improving the overall user experience. These iterations were essential in fine-tuning the app to meet the needs of its users.

Challenges:

One of the most significant challenges in designing Centsafe was striking the right balance between simplicity and functionality. Users needed a tool that was powerful enough to manage their finances but simple enough to use without frustration. The autosave feature, in particular, required careful consideration—users had to feel comfortable setting up automatedsavings without worrying about overcommitting. To address this, I focused on providing clear, step-by-step instructions and options to pause or adjust the autosave settings, ensuring flexibility and control.

What Went Well:

The development of Centsafe successfully translated user needs into a streamlined, user-friendly financial tool. The clean design, combined with intuitive features like Auto-Save, received positive feedback for its simplicity and effectiveness in helping users reach their savings goals.

What Didn't Go Well:

In the early stages, balancing the app's simplicity with the inclusion of advanced features proved difficult. Some initial designs were too complex for the target audience, requiring multiple revisions to achieve the right level of clarity and accessibility. Additionally, the time constraints limited the depth of analytics and educational features that could be implemented.

Key Learnings:

The key takeaway for me was- designing for simplicity without sacrificing functionality is crucial, especially for users who may be less familiar with financial tools. I learned that iterative testing and user feedback are essential to refining the user experience and ensuring the app meets users' needs. Additionally, clear communication of financial concepts through intuitive design is vital in building user confidence and trust.

If I Had More Time:

If I were to revisit this project, I would explore ways to enhance the app’s educational aspects, perhaps by incorporating more interactive elements that explain financial concepts in simple terms. Additionally, I see potential in expanding the app’s analytics features to offer predictive insights, helping users anticipate financial trends and adjust their savings strategies accordingly.
